# HERRERA ATTEND Privacy Notice

Last updated: July 20, 2026

HERRERA ATTEND is an employer-provided attendance and employee-services application. This draft must be completed with the employer's legal identity, contact details, retention periods and production service providers before publication.

## Information processed

The application may process employee identity and contact details, employee number, worksite and schedule, precise or approximate location captured during an attendance action, clock timestamps and attendance decisions, device-registration information, leave and other employee requests, notifications, and payroll or payslip information.

## Why information is processed

Information is used to authenticate employees, register authorized devices, verify attendance at an assigned worksite, maintain attendance and scheduling records, process employee requests, provide payroll information, prevent misuse, resolve disputes, meet employer obligations and protect the service.

## Sharing

Information is available to the employer and authorized personnel according to their roles. It may be processed by contracted hosting, identity, security or support providers. The final published notice must identify relevant categories of recipients and any international transfers.

## Retention and deletion

Records are retained according to the employer's documented employment, payroll, tax, security and legal requirements. Employees may contact **[PRIVACY CONTACT EMAIL]** to request access, correction or deletion where applicable. Some records may need to be retained when required by law or legitimate employment obligations.

## Security

The production application requires encrypted HTTPS connections, uses secure device storage for session information, restricts payroll and attendance access by identity and role, and records security-relevant actions. No system can guarantee absolute security.

## Location permissions

Location is requested to verify an attendance action against the assigned worksite. The current Android application does not request background-location permission. Denying location prevents GPS-verified clocking but does not by itself delete existing employment records.
